# Go-To-Market Plan

## Exact Target User

The first user I would target is an engineering manager, CTO, or lead developer at a seed to Series A software startup with 8-40 engineers and at least `$2,000/month` of AI-tool or model-credit spend. The highest-intent version is a team already paying for Claude, OpenAI, or Gemini usage and regularly approving additional credits when limits are reached.

This is narrower than "startups." My second interview described an 11-person engineering team using Claude Enterprise that reaches limits weekly and estimates `$3,000-$5,000/month` in extra credits. That user has both the technical context to complete an audit and a manager who can approve action.

## The Moment They Need SpendLens

They are unlikely to search for an audit tool casually. They need it after:

- A manager asks why the AI bill grew again.
- A team reaches an enterprise usage limit and requests more credits.
- A founder sees multiple coding-assistant subscriptions on one expense report.
- An engineering lead is asked whether cutting cost will slow a release.

Searches or posts near that moment may include `"Claude enterprise usage limits"`, `"reduce OpenAI API cost"`, `"Cursor vs Copilot team pricing"`, or complaints about AI credit burn in founder and engineering communities.

## Where I Would Reach Them

| Channel | Specific Approach | Why It Fits |
| --- | --- | --- |
| LinkedIn founder/engineering posts | Post a real anonymized example: an enterprise team hitting limits weekly and what a 10% credit saving means. Direct-message engineering leads who comment on AI spend. | Budget owners and technical recommenders are both present. |
| Hacker News `Show HN` | Launch the public tool with the rule-based pricing explanation and invite criticism of the assumptions. | Technical users will test credibility, and public audit links are shareable. |
| Focused Reddit discussions | Contribute to existing AI tooling/cost threads in `r/ClaudeAI`, `r/ChatGPTCoding`, and `r/startups` when relevant, linking only when it answers the question. | Users already discuss limits, plans, and tool overlap. |
| Credex network | Send a benchmark-based audit link to existing prospects asking about AI credits, and let account conversations surface anonymized use cases. | Credex has the most relevant distribution advantage: people already discussing credit purchasing. |

## First 100 Completed Audits In 30 Days

| Activity | Target Completed Audits |
| --- | ---: |
| One `Show HN` launch plus follow-up answers | 30 |
| Four LinkedIn posts with one concrete calculation each | 25 |
| Direct outreach to 60 engineering leads who mention AI spend or limits | 15 |
| Relevant Reddit conversations and founder community replies | 10 |
| Credex sharing the tool with existing high-intent contacts | 20 |
| **Total** | **100** |

I would not post generic promotional links everywhere. Each post would show one honest calculation and link to a public report so people can judge the output before giving an email.

## Unfair Distribution Channel

Credex can use conversations it already has about AI credits to distribute an audit whose next step is directly relevant: verifying whether repeated credit purchases can be obtained more efficiently. A generic SaaS founder can publish an AI-cost calculator; Credex can connect a high-savings result to an actual credit-purchasing conversation.

## Week-One Traction Signal

In the first week, success would look like `150` visitors, `45` completed audits, `12` emails captured, at least `5` audits showing more than `$500/month` savings, and `2` booked credit-review conversations. If completed audits happen but no high-savings user requests a review, the product is interesting content but not yet a lead channel.
